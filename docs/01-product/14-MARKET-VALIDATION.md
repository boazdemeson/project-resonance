# Market Validation Plan

## Purpose

00-VISION through 10-JOBS-TO-BE-DONE are built almost entirely on hypothesis (a single, well-drawn persona — Alex — but no evidence of real musician input). Before Phase 1 build effort scales up, the riskiest assumptions should be tested cheaply. This document lists them in priority order and how to test each.

---

# Riskiest Assumptions, Ranked

## 1. "Musicians will adopt a new tool at all" (highest risk)

The Problem doc correctly identifies the status quo (Drive + WhatsApp + Discord) as painful — but painful ≠ someone will switch. Tool-switching for an active creative workflow has real friction cost, especially mid-project.

**Test:** Show 10–15 working independent musicians (ideally a mix of solo producers and small collaborating groups) an actual clickable prototype of the Session/Snapshot/Timeline flow. Ask them to walk through their *last real song* using it instead of describing it hypothetically. Watch for hesitation points, not just stated enthusiasm — stated interest in concept interviews is a weak signal.

## 2. "The Session model beats folders/WhatsApp in practice, not just on paper"

**Test:** A/B a real task — e.g., "find the approved final mix from three weeks ago and the feedback that led to it" — once in their current tools, once in the prototype. Time it. If the prototype isn't faster or clearly less error-prone, the core differentiation claim in 06-VALUE-PROPOSITION doesn't hold.

## 3. "Non-technical collaborators will actually leave timestamped comments in a new tool instead of just texting"

This is JTBD 6 and one of the "Magic Features" (Time-based Comments). It's untested and it's core.

**Test:** Give a musician's actual collaborator (singer, mixing engineer, bandmate) a real Snapshot and ask them to give feedback both ways — voice note vs. in-app timestamped comment. See which one they reach for unprompted on the second ask.

## 4. Willingness to pay

**Test:** Don't ask "would you pay for this" (unreliable). Instead, after the prototype walkthrough, present the free-tier storage cap and paid tier price point from 12-BUSINESS-MODEL and gauge real reaction — does the cap feel fair, or does it immediately trigger "I'll just keep using Drive"?

---

# Suggested Validation Sequence

1. **5 informal interviews** with independent musicians about current workflow (validate 01-PROBLEM's framing directly — is the fragmentation actually the top pain, or is it something else entirely, like getting collaborators to respond at all?).
2. **Clickable prototype** (even Figma-level, doesn't need to be functional) of Create Session → Upload Snapshot → Timeline → Comment.
3. **5–10 task-based usability sessions** against assumption #1 and #2 above.
4. **Paid pilot** with 2–3 real in-progress projects (a working solo artist, a working duo/band) using it for an actual song end-to-end, tied to the M1/M2 roadmap milestones.

This sequence can realistically run in parallel with early Phase 1 build work rather than blocking it — but the pricing and free-tier cap decisions in 12-BUSINESS-MODEL should be treated as provisional until step 4 produces real signal.

---

## Decisions

- Validation should center on task-based comparison (current workflow vs. prototype), not opinion-gathering — stated interest is not a reliable signal for creative tool adoption.
- The single persona (Alex) should be checked against at least a few real musicians before Phase 2 (Collaboration) investment, since collaboration-specific JTBDs (6, and the "Magic Features") are the least validated parts of the whole doc set.
- Pricing (12-BUSINESS-MODEL) should be treated as a hypothesis, adjusted after pilot step 4.

## Open Questions

- Does "independent musician" need segmentation (bedroom producer vs. gigging band vs. semi-pro with a small team)? Alex as drawn may blend personas with meaningfully different needs and willingness to pay.
